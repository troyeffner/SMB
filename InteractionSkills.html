Your team knows

Most organizational problems are not information problems. Your people already know that handoffs need context, that decisions need commitment, and that alignment requires acknowledgment. They just don't have the behavioral muscle memory to do it automatically, under pressure, in real time.

We use genuinely fun, fast-moving exercises in a low-stakes environment where people laugh, get things wrong, and try again. That environment is not incidental. It is the whole mechanism. People practice new behaviors when it feels safe to be imperfect. The skills that emerge in the room are the same ones they need back at their desks.

01 — The Problem

Why training doesn't stick — and what
actually changes behavior

Organizations spend enormous amounts on workshops, frameworks, and documentation. The knowledge transfer works. The behavioral change doesn't. That gap is the problem we solve.

"When people are laughing and moving and getting things slightly wrong in a room together, their guard comes down. That is exactly when new behaviors get practiced and retained. The fun is not a reward for attending. It is how the learning works."

02 — Applications

Ten problems.
Ten targeted interventions.

Each session begins with identifying where the breakdown is actually happening. Then we design a session built around exercises that are engaging and energetic, targeted to the specific behavioral skill your team is missing. People leave having genuinely enjoyed the hour. They also leave with a concrete new skill.

01

Alignment & Getting on the Same Page

Sales promises what ops can't deliver. Leadership disagrees on priorities. Teams duplicate work nobody coordinated. The fix isn't another meeting. It's practicing explicit acknowledgment before contribution.

02

Process Breakdowns & Bottlenecks

Simple things take too long. Approvals sit. Requests bounce. The root cause is almost always observation interpreted as action before the facts are actually on the table.

03

Handoffs & Communication Gaps

Information gets lost between people and teams. Nobody knows who follows up. The same questions get asked at every step. People don't know how to set up the next person for success.

04

People Working Around the System

Separate spreadsheets. Informal phone calls. Skipped steps. Workarounds exist because the official system doesn't match reality. The goal isn't compliance. It's understanding what the workaround is telling you.

05

Scaling & Growth Pains

What worked at 10 people breaks at 30. The owner used to hold it all together. Now things slip. The problem is distributing knowledge and decision-making that was never made explicit.

06

Technology & System Integration

Data entered in three places. Systems that don't talk. Software nobody uses. Technology problems are almost always workflow problems in disguise. You can't fix the system until you observe the actual workflow.

07

Priorities & Decision-Making

Everything feels urgent. Nothing gets finished. Analysis paralysis while the window closes. The skill that's missing isn't better data. It's commitment with incomplete information and fast recovery when wrong.

08

Customer or Quality Issues

Recurring complaints that training doesn't fix. Quality that varies by who does the work. The gap between what you think you deliver and what the customer receives is almost always an intent-versus-impact problem.

09

New Hire Onboarding

New people taking months to get up to speed. Everything relies on one trainer. Critical knowledge lives in one person's head and never survives the transfer. Tacit knowledge has to be made explicit before it can travel.

10

Unclear Roles & Responsibilities

Tasks fall through because everyone assumed someone else would handle it. Duplicate work. Ownership battles. The fix isn't an org chart. It's building ambient awareness of whose turn it is and when to step back.

03 — Method

Built backward from
the business problem.

Every session is designed in the same order: identify the dysfunction, select exercises that are genuinely fun and fast-moving, practice the specific skill repeatedly in a room where imperfection is safe, then build the transfer plan back to real work. The exercises earn the learning. The debrief makes it stick.

1

Diagnose the actual dysfunction

We start with discovery questions that expose where the behavioral breakdown is actually happening, not where leadership thinks it's happening. Most organizations misdiagnose: they describe symptoms as the problem when the behavior causing them is three steps upstream.

Pre-session
2

Practice the missing behavior in a room that makes it feel safe

The exercises are fast, energetic, and genuinely fun. People laugh. They get things wrong. They try again without penalty. That environment is the point. New behaviors require psychological safety to take root, and these exercises create it quickly. Participants practice the specific skill your organization is missing, repeatedly, in a context where imperfection is expected and recovery is celebrated.

The session
3

Transfer the skill to a specific real situation

Every session ends with a concrete transfer plan: one specific situation in the coming week where the practiced skill applies. Then 1-week, 1-month, and 3-month check-ins that reinforce application and surface the next layer of behavioral work.

05 — Formats

Three ways to
engage.

Every format includes pre-session diagnosis, targeted exercise selection, structured debrief, transfer planning, and follow-up reinforcement. The difference is scope and depth.

90 Minutes

Single Problem Session

A focused intervention for one specific organizational dysfunction. Ideal for teams that have already identified a clear behavioral problem and want to address it directly.

  • Pre-session diagnostic call (30 min)
  • One primary exercise, one supporting exercise
  • Structured debrief and transfer planning
  • 1-week follow-up check-in
  • Best for groups of 6 to 20
Most Common Half Day

Multi-Problem Workshop

A deeper session that addresses two to three interconnected dysfunctions. Most organizational problems cluster together, and the most effective interventions address the pattern, not just one symptom.

  • Pre-session diagnostic interviews (60 min)
  • Foundation, core, and application exercises
  • Cross-dysfunction pattern mapping
  • Individual and team transfer planning
  • 1-week and 1-month follow-up
  • Best for groups of 8 to 30
3 Months

Sustained Skills Program

A full behavioral change engagement. Three sessions, spaced across a quarter, that build foundation skills and then progressively develop more advanced behaviors as the team integrates what they practiced.

  • Full organizational diagnosis across all 10 categories
  • Session 1: Foundation skills and safety
  • Session 2: Advanced skills and application
  • Session 3: Integration and organizational embedding
  • Monthly check-ins and measurement
  • Written behavioral change summary
